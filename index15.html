Operating as a single entity principle: Business and personal finances should always be separated. In the eyes of the law, a business and its owner are two separate entities. Specific currency principle: All amounts in the financial statement are expressed in the same currency. In the US, this means that all numbers are expressed in US dollars. If a transaction occurs in a foreign currency, it must be converted to achieve consistency. Specific time period principle: Financial statements should clearly define the time period they cover. They must have a start and end date. Historical Cost Principle: When considering historical costs, factors such as inflation and stagnation are not taken into account. All costs are stated at their actual amounts, not their relative value
